# Etiquette Checklist 

## ✅ The "Do's" of Social Media

- Take ten minutes every semester to ensure you know exactly who can see your tagged photos and personal posts.

- Use platforms like LinkedIn to showcase your projects, internships, and academic achievements.

- Check the credibility of news or viral information before hitting "repost" to avoid spreading misinformation.

- Follow alumni, industry leaders, and campus organizations to stay updated on career opportunities.

- Share content that highlights your passions, volunteer work, or positive contributions to your community.

## ❌ The "Don'ts" of Social Media

 - Avoid "venting" about professors, difficult exams, or employers in the heat of the moment.

 - Keep your specific dorm location, class schedule, or student ID numbers off your public stories.

 - If someone is being toxic or baiting you into an argument, use the block or report button instead of responding.

 - Avoid posting evidence of underage drinking, illegal activities, or anything that suggests academic dishonesty.

 - Never assume a post is temporary or hidden; always act as if a future employer could see a screenshot of it.
